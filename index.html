Jim Rohn

The Great Mentor and Business Philosopher

Jim Rohn was known for his wit, wisdom and easy-going personality.

"[Jim Rohn] is among the most polished, professional speakers in America, with a message everyone should hear." —Brian Tracy

A Short Timeline of Jim Rohn's Life

  • 1930 - Born in Yakima, Washington; at some point, Rohn's parents moved to their farm in Caldwell, Idaho, where he grew up as an only child.
  • 1955 - At the peak of his weariness, Rohn met his soon-to-be mentor—an entrepreneur named Earl Schoaff; up to this point, Rohn had gotten married, dropped out of college after only one year, and was struggling financially; Rohn joined one of Schoaff's direct selling businesses.
  • 1957 - Rohn moved to another one of Schoaff's businesses, where Schoaff began mentoring him, teaching him about business and personal development.
  • 1960 - Schoaff's business expanded internationally, and Rohn was selected as the company's vice president.
  • 1963 - Rohn gave his first public seminar; up to this point, Rohn had already given a few private speeches at the Rotary Club, high schools, and service clubs. His first public seminar at the Beverly Hills Hotel launched him into the personal development business. He soon after gave seminars all over the country, and eventually wrote several books.
  • 1970s - Rohn became a mentor to other entrepreneurs and life-coaches, including Mark Hughes and Tony Robbins. Many others also credited Rohn for his influence on the careers, including Mark Victor Hansen, Jack Canfield, Brian Tracy, Todd Smith, T. Harv Eker, and others.
  • 1985 - Rohn received the National Speakers Association CPAE Award for excellence in speaking.
  • 2009 - Rohn died of pulmonary fibrosis, and was buried in Forest Lawn Memorial Park in Glendale, CA. His personal development career spanded over 40 years, including giving seminars worldwide, writing books, and recording audio and video material. His influence continues today, as millions still seek out his books, recordings, and mentorship from those that he mentored.
